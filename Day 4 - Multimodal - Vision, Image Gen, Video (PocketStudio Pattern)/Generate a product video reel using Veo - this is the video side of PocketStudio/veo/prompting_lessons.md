# Veo Prompting Lessons

## 1. Use Cinematic Camera Language

Good prompts include:
- slow dolly-in
- tracking shot
- low-angle shot
- aerial shot
- handheld motion

This dramatically improves realism.

---

## 2. Lighting Keywords Matter

Lighting strongly changes quality.

Useful phrases:
- golden hour lighting
- cinematic shadows
- rim lighting
- neon edge lighting
- soft studio lighting

Avoid vague terms like:
- "good lighting"
- "nice lighting"

---

## 3. Add Depth and Atmosphere

Words like:
- atmospheric haze
- shallow depth of field
- volumetric lighting
- reflections

help scenes feel more cinematic.

---

## 4. Control Motion Explicitly

Without motion instructions, videos may feel robotic.

Useful motion phrases:
- smooth slow-motion rotation
- subtle handheld movement
- cinematic pacing
- gradual reveal

---

## 5. Keep Scene Composition Focused

Too many background elements distract from the subject.

Keep:
- one clear hero object
- simple environments
- controlled colors

---

## 6. Use Advertisement References Carefully

Examples:
- Nike-style commercial
- luxury automotive ad aesthetic
- Apple keynote visual style

This helps Veo understand tone and polish level.

---

## 7. Iterate Aggressively

The first generation is rarely the best.

Workflow:
- Generate
- Critique specific problems
- Rewrite prompt precisely
- Regenerate

Iteration quality matters more than initial prompting.
